DFG SCHWAN Project · #490909448

Automated Behavioral Coding of Early Parent-Infant Interaction

The SCHWAN project develops computational methods to support Infant and Caregiver Engagement Phases–Revised (ICEP-R) coding in the Face-to-Face Still-Face paradigm - from frozen foundation-model features to fine-tuned multimodal large language models - as scalable, expert-in-the-loop annotation support for developmental and clinical research.

Publications

ICMI 2026 · Napoli, Italy

Automated Behavioural Analysis in Parent-Infant Interactions using Multimodal-Large-Language Models

Controlled comparison of zero-shot prompting, frozen-feature transfer, and LoRA fine-tuning of MLLMs (Qwen2.5-Omni, Qwen2-Audio, Qwen3-VL, GLM-4.6) for ICEP-R coding. Fine-tuned Qwen2.5-Omni-7B reaches weighted F1 of 0.657 (infant) / 0.692 (caregiver), clearly outperforming zero-shot MLLMs and strong DINOv3 + Wav2Vec 2.0 frozen-feature baselines.

ICMI 2024 · San Jose, Costa Rica

Towards Automated Annotation of Infant-Caregiver Engagement Phases with Multimodal Foundation Models

The predecessor work: ICEP-R prediction from frozen DINOv2 (vision) and Wav2Vec2-BERT (audio) embeddings with bidirectional LSTM and linear downstream classifiers, integrated with the NOVA / DISCOVER annotation ecosystem.
